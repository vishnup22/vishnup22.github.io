About

Publications

How Fast Can Reward Models Score? A Systems Study of C++ and PyTorch Inference Runtimes for RLHF
Vishnu Pulipaka, Anish Katta, Deva Rohit Reddy Peddireddy. arXiv:2607.19712, 2026.

Built a native C++ inference engine on ONNX Runtime for RLHF reward-model scoring and benchmarked it against PyTorch eager mode, torch.compile, and FastAPI. On CPU the C++ engine wins outright; on GPU, torch.compile wins. The CPU speedup turned out to come from ONNX Runtime's graph execution, not from C++ itself, and batching strategy mattered more than either.

arXiv Code
[Title withheld — under double-blind review]
Vishnu Pulipaka. Submitted, under double-blind review.

A release-level fairness drift audit framework applied to 12 model versions from 5 providers across 6 model families and 7 bias benchmarks (about 200K examples). Toxicity declines slightly on average but the confidence interval spans zero; stereotype scores hold steady or worsen in 67% of version transitions, with one statistically significant regression in Gemini-2.5-Pro. All findings replicate after excluding the two most criticized benchmarks.

Code
[Title withheld — under review]
Vishnu Pulipaka. Submitted to the BabyLM Workshop, under review.

Translates the 100M-word English BabyLM corpus into Hindi and Telugu with IndicTrans2, then trains and evaluates monolingual and bilingual GPT-Wee and GPT-BERT models. GPT-BERT consistently outperforms GPT-Wee, but models trained on translated data generalize worse than those trained on native CC-100 data when tested out of domain, which matters more than the in-domain perplexity numbers suggest.
